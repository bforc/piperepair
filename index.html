Why Traditional Repairs Fall Short

In older buildings, especially those constructed before modern PVC piping became standard, downspouts often consist of iron, lead, or ceramic materials. These are prone to corrosion, cracking, or leaking over time. Traditionally, fixing these issues meant breaking into walls or ceilings, replacing the pipes manually, then rebuilding and restoring the affected areas. This process is:

  • Expensive

  • Noisy and messy

With Entuba-s approach, these inconveniences are avoided altogether.

How Entuba Repairs Downspouts Without Construction

Entuba uses a method based on Cured-In-Place Pipe (CIPP) lining, a trenchless technology originally developed for large sewer systems. Here-s how the process works step-by-step:

  1. Inspection
    Using state-of-the-art video cameras, Entuba inspects the inside of the downspouts to assess the extent of damage and locate issues like cracks, leaks, or obstructions.

  2. Cleaning
    The pipe is thoroughly cleaned with high-pressure water or mechanical tools to remove any grease, scale, or buildup that could interfere with the lining process.

  3. Liner Installation
    A flexible resin-impregnated liner is inserted into the pipe using compressed air or water. This liner molds to the internal shape of the existing pipe.

  4. Curing
    The liner is hardened in place using hot water, steam, or UV light, creating a solid, seamless, and durable new pipe within the old one.

  5. Final Inspection
    After curing, Entuba conducts a second camera inspection to ensure the pipe is fully sealed and functioning properly.

This method reinforces the structural integrity of the existing pipe while preventing leaks, root intrusion, or corrosion.
